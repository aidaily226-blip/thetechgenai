## Display Comparison

All four phones have AMOLED displays with 120Hz refresh rate — which means smooth scrolling on all of them. But there are important differences.

The **Realme 16 Pro+** has the best display of the four. Its Snapdragon 7 Gen 4 chip handles graphics better which makes the display feel more responsive during fast-moving content like gaming and videos.

The **OnePlus Nord CE5** display is slightly smaller at 6.74 inches but has excellent colour accuracy. OxygenOS also handles display calibration well so colours look natural and not oversaturated.

The **OPPO K13** display is good for the price — ₹17,441 for an AMOLED 120Hz display is genuinely impressive. But the peak brightness is lower than the Realme 16 Pro models.

**Display Winner: Realme 16 Pro+** — best brightness and smoothness overall.

## Camera Test — Real World Samples

The camera is where these phones differ the most. Here is what each camera actually produces in different conditions.

### Daylight Photography

**Realme 16 Pro and Pro+ — 200MP main camera**

The 200MP sensor on both Realme 16 models is the biggest talking point. In good daylight, photos are incredibly detailed. You can zoom into a photo and read text that was barely visible to your eyes. Trees, buildings, and street scenes have excellent sharpness.

The OIS (Optical Image Stabilisation) means photos are sharp even if your hand moves slightly while pressing the shutter. For 200MP photos this matters a lot — at that resolution even small movements create blur.

However — most people never actually shoot in 200MP mode because the file sizes are enormous (around 40-80MB per photo). The default mode shoots in a lower resolution that still uses the full sensor for better quality. Results are very good but not dramatically better than 50MP competitors in normal daylight.

**OnePlus Nord CE5 — 50MP Sony LYT-600**

The Sony LYT-600 sensor is considered one of the best 50MP sensors available in 2026. Sony sensors are known for natural colour reproduction — skin tones look realistic rather than overly smoothed or artificially brightened.

In daylight, the Nord CE5 produces photos that look the most natural and closest to what your eyes actually see. The dynamic range — showing both bright sky and dark shadows in the same photo — is excellent.

**OPPO K13 — 50MP standard sensor**

The K13's 50MP camera is decent for the price. Daylight photos are sharp and colourful. However the processing is more aggressive — colours are boosted and skin is smoothed more than necessary. Photos look impressive on the phone screen but less realistic when viewed on a computer.

**Daylight Camera Winner: OnePlus Nord CE5** — most natural and accurate. Realme 16 Pro/Pro+ win on detail and zoom ability.

---

### Night Photography

Night photography is where camera quality differences become very obvious.

**Realme 16 Pro / Pro+** — Night mode is good but not exceptional. The 200MP sensor is large which helps capture more light. With night mode on, photos are bright and reasonably detailed. However in very dark conditions there is noticeable noise (grain) in shadow areas.

**OnePlus Nord CE5** — Night photography is genuinely impressive for this price. The Sony LYT-600 sensor handles low light better than expected. Night mode photos have less noise, more natural colours, and better detail in dark areas. This is one of the Nord CE5's biggest strengths.

**OPPO K13** — Night photography is the weakest of the four. Photos become noticeably noisy in low light and colours become inaccurate. Acceptable for casual sharing but not for anyone who frequently shoots in the evening.

**Night Camera Winner: OnePlus Nord CE5** — clearly best low light performance.

### BGMI (Battlegrounds Mobile India) Test

**Realme 16 Pro+ (Snapdragon 7 Gen 4)**
BGMI runs at HDR + Ultra frame rate settings smoothly. In intense firefights with multiple players, frame drops are minimal. The Snapdragon chip handles graphics consistently. After 45 minutes of continuous gaming the phone became warm but not hot. Gaming experience is excellent.

**OnePlus Nord CE5 (Dimensity 8350 Apex)**
Very close to the Pro+ in BGMI performance. Also handles HDR + Ultra frame rate well. The Dimensity 8350 is a powerful chip and the phone stays cool during gaming better than the Pro+. For gamers this phone is excellent considering its ₹24,999 price.

**Realme 16 Pro (Dimensity 7300 Max)**
BGMI runs at HD + High frame rate settings comfortably. Ultra frame rate at HDR causes occasional frame drops during intense scenes. Good for casual gaming but serious gamers will notice the difference versus the Pro+.

**OPPO K13 (Snapdragon 6 Gen 4)**
BGMI at HD + High frame rate is comfortable. Ultra settings cause visible stuttering. For Free Fire and casual gaming the K13 is fine. For serious BGMI competitive play it struggles in complex scenes.

### Free Fire Test

All four phones handle Free Fire at maximum settings without issues. Free Fire is less demanding than BGMI so even the K13 runs it smoothly.

## Battery Life and Charging Test

All four phones have massive batteries — 7000mAh or more. This is one of the biggest selling points of this entire lineup.

### Screen On Time (Real World Usage)

Screen on time means how many hours the screen is actually on — browsing, watching, gaming. This is the most realistic battery test.

| Phone | Screen On Time | Full Charge Time |
|-------|:-------------:|:----------------:|
| Realme 16 Pro | 11-13 hours | 58 minutes |
| Realme 16 Pro+ | 10-12 hours | 55 minutes |
| OPPO K13 | 12-14 hours 🏆 | 56 minutes |
| OnePlus Nord CE5 | 10-12 hours | 54 minutes |

### Why OPPO K13 Has Best Battery Life?

Interesting result — the cheapest phone actually has the best battery life. The reason is the Snapdragon 6 Gen 4 chip is more power efficient than the more powerful chips in the Realme 16 Pro and Pro+. Less powerful chip = less battery consumption = longer life.

The Realme 16 Pro+ has the most powerful chip and therefore uses battery fastest despite having the same 7000mAh capacity.

### Charging Test — All Four Phones Have 80W

All four phones charge at 80W which means:

```
0% to 50% → approximately 25-28 minutes
0% to 100% → approximately 55-60 minutes
```

This is genuinely fast. Plug in for 30 minutes while getting ready in the morning and you have more than half a battery. Full overnight charging is not necessary with these phones.

**Battery Winner: OPPO K13** — longest battery life.
**Charging: All four tied** — same 80W speed.

## FAQs

**Which Realme 16 phone is best?**
Realme 16 Pro+ for performance and gaming. Realme 16 Pro for camera and better value.

**Is Realme 16 Pro+ good for gaming?**
Yes — excellent. AnTuTu score near 950,000 puts it in the high-end gaming category. BGMI runs at HDR Ultra settings comfortably.

**Which phone is best under ₹25,000 in India 2026?**
OnePlus Nord CE5 is the clear winner — best camera, 4 years of updates, and good gaming performance.

**Is Realme 16 Pro worth buying over OnePlus Nord CE5?**
If you specifically want 200MP photos and 4K video — yes. For everything else the Nord CE5 offers better value at ₹7,000 less.

**Which phone has the best battery?**
OPPO K13 gives the most screen-on-time at 12-14 hours despite being the cheapest phone. All four charge at the same 80W speed.

**Is the 200MP camera on Realme 16 Pro actually useful?**
For detail, zoom, and cropping flexibility — yes. For casual daily photography the difference versus 50MP is smaller than you might expect. The Sony LYT-600 sensor in the Nord CE5 produces more natural results in most situations.
