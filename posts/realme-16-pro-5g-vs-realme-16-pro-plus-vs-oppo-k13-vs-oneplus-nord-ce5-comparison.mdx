## Processor — Performance Comparison

This is where the four phones differ the most significantly.

### Realme 16 Pro — MediaTek Dimensity 7300 Max
The Dimensity 7300 Max handles everyday tasks well but shows weaker benchmark scores compared to competitors in this price range. Real world performance is smooth for daily use but it tends to get warm during heavy gaming sessions like BGMI and COD Mobile.

### Realme 16 Pro+ — Snapdragon 7 Gen 4
A significant upgrade over the standard model. The Snapdragon 7 Gen 4 is a genuine mid-range powerhouse that handles everything smoothly — heavy gaming, multitasking, camera processing. This is the biggest reason to spend the extra money on the Pro+.

### OPPO K13 — Snapdragon 6 Gen 4
Perfectly adequate for everyday tasks at its price point. Do not expect it to match the Realme or OnePlus phones in heavy gaming, but for WhatsApp, Instagram, YouTube, and casual games it works perfectly well.

### OnePlus Nord CE5 — Dimensity 8350 Apex
Surprisingly powerful for its price. The Dimensity 8350 Apex punches well above its weight and delivers near-flagship performance in benchmarks. For gaming and multitasking, the Nord CE5 offers exceptional value.

**Winner:** Realme 16 Pro+ (Snapdragon 7 Gen 4) for heavy users, OnePlus Nord CE5 for best value performance

## Camera — 200MP vs 50MP

The most talked-about feature of the Realme 16 series is the 200MP camera. But does it actually matter?

### Realme 16 Pro and 16 Pro+ — 200MP Main Camera
Both Realme phones pack a 200MP primary camera — the highest resolution in this comparison by far. In daylight conditions, the 200MP sensor captures incredible detail. You can zoom into photos and crop aggressively without losing quality.

However, the large sensor can struggle in very low light, and the AI processing sometimes produces unnatural looking results. The 8MP ultrawide is decent but nothing exceptional.

### OPPO K13 — 50MP Main Camera
A competent 50MP camera that performs reliably in daylight. Night mode is average. The 2MP depth sensor is essentially useless — this is the weakest camera setup of the four phones.

### OnePlus Nord CE5 — 50MP Sony LYT-600 Sensor
OnePlus has used a premium Sony LYT-600 sensor for the Nord CE5. Despite being 50MP, the larger sensor size and better optics mean this camera can outperform the Realme phones in low light conditions. The 8MP ultrawide is also more capable than the OPPO K13's 2MP sensor.

**Winner:** Realme 16 Pro / 16 Pro+ for maximum resolution and detail in daylight. OnePlus Nord CE5 for best overall camera experience including low light.

All four phones offer enough battery for a full day of heavy use and easily last two days for light users. The OnePlus Nord CE5 has a slightly larger 7100mAh battery but the difference is negligible in real life.

## Final Verdict

Each phone serves a different type of buyer:

**Best camera phone:** Realme 16 Pro / 16 Pro+ — The 200MP camera is genuinely impressive for daylight photography
