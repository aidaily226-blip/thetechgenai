Something big just happened in the AI world.

On April 8, 2026, Meta — the company behind Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp — launched a brand new AI model called **Muse Spark**.

Not an update. Not a small improvement. A completely new AI — built from scratch over nine months by a team called Meta Superintelligence Labs.

And Meta is not being shy about what it wants.

They are calling Muse Spark the first step toward **personal superintelligence**.

That is a massive claim. So let us break down exactly what Muse Spark is, how it compares to ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude AI — and whether the hype is real.

---

## First — What Is Meta Muse Spark?

Muse Spark is Meta's newest and most powerful AI model.

Think of it like Meta's version of ChatGPT. You can talk to it, ask it questions, show it photos, and get help with all kinds of tasks.

But unlike ChatGPT, Muse Spark is built directly into the apps you already use every day.

It is already live in the Meta AI app and on meta.ai right now. And in the coming weeks, it will roll out inside WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, Messenger, and even Meta's AI glasses.

That means the next time you open WhatsApp and tap the Meta AI button — you are talking to Muse Spark.

### Who Built It?

Muse Spark was built by **Meta Superintelligence Labs** — a new division inside Meta headed by **Alexandr Wang**.

Alexandr Wang was the founder and CEO of Scale AI — one of the most important data companies in the AI world, valued at billions of dollars. Meta paid an enormous amount to bring him in nine months ago. Muse Spark is his first major delivery.

The model was code-named **Avocado** during development. Now it is the foundation of everything Meta is building in AI going forward.

---

## What Can Meta Muse Spark Actually Do?

Here is where it gets interesting.

Muse Spark is not just a chatbot. It has several different modes and abilities that set it apart.

### Two Modes — Fast and Thinking

Muse Spark has two ways of working:

**Instant Mode** — For quick, everyday questions. Ask something simple and get a fast answer. Like ChatGPT's standard mode.

**Thinking Mode** — For harder, more complex questions. The AI takes more time and really reasons through the problem step by step. Like ChatGPT's o1 reasoning mode or Claude AI's extended thinking.

You can switch between them depending on what you need.

### It Can See the World — Not Just Read Text

This is one of Muse Spark's biggest strengths.

Most AI models work best when you type them a question. Muse Spark was built to understand photos and the real world around you.

Snap a photo of a snack shelf at the airport and ask which snack has the most protein. Muse Spark can look at the labels and tell you — without you having to type out every item.

Scan a product in a shop and ask how it compares to similar products. It can analyse and answer.

This is called **multimodal perception** — the ability to understand words AND images together.

And when Muse Spark rolls out to Meta's AI glasses, this becomes even more powerful. The AI will be able to see whatever you are looking at in real life and respond to it.

### Multiple AI Agents Working at the Same Time

Here is something genuinely new.

When you ask Muse Spark a complex question — like planning a family holiday — it does not do everything one step at a time. It launches multiple **subagents** working in parallel.

One agent plans the itinerary. Another compares locations. A third finds activities for kids. All at the same time.

The result? Faster, more complete answers than a single AI working alone.

This is what is called **agentic AI** — AI that can break down a task and assign different parts to different agents automatically.

### Shopping Mode — Built Into Instagram and Facebook

Muse Spark has something none of the other major AIs have.

A dedicated **shopping mode**.

Because Meta owns Instagram and Facebook — where millions of people post about products, fashion, and brands — Muse Spark can tap into all of that content to give you shopping recommendations.

Ask what to wear for a beach holiday. Ask what gift to buy for someone. Ask how to style a room.

Muse Spark surfaces ideas from the creators and communities people already follow on Instagram and Facebook. Not generic results — personalised suggestions based on what you and your community actually like.

No other AI — not ChatGPT, not Gemini, not Claude AI — can do this. Because no other AI has access to Instagram's and Facebook's social data.

### Health Questions

Health is one of the most common things people ask AI about.

Muse Spark was specifically designed to handle health questions better than before. Meta worked with a team of physicians to develop this capability. It can respond to detailed health queries — including some that involve photos of charts or medical images.

## How Is Meta Muse Spark Different From ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude AI?

This is the question everyone is asking.

Let us compare them honestly.

---

### Meta Muse Spark vs ChatGPT

**ChatGPT** is made by OpenAI. It is the most famous AI in the world — 900 million weekly users. It is incredibly powerful. GPT-5 is one of the most capable AI models available today.

But ChatGPT is a standalone product. You go to chat.openai.com or the app and use it there. It does not live inside your daily apps.

**Muse Spark** is built directly into WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, and Messenger. You do not have to go anywhere new. It is already where you are.

The other big difference — ChatGPT has no access to your social world. It does not know what your friends are posting. It does not know what creators you follow. Muse Spark does.

**In coding and overall intelligence:** ChatGPT and GPT-5 are still ahead. Meta itself admits there is currently a gap between Muse Spark and the top models in coding and some advanced tasks.

**In social integration and shopping:** Muse Spark wins. Nothing else comes close.

---

### Meta Muse Spark vs Gemini

**Gemini** is Google's AI. In 2026, Gemini 3 is actually ranked number one in performance on several benchmarks. It is deeply integrated into Google Search, Gmail, Google Docs, and Android.

The comparison between Muse Spark and Gemini is actually quite similar — both are trying to be the AI that lives inside the services you already use, rather than being a separate product.

Google has Search. Meta has social media.

If you want AI help inside Google's world — Gemini. If you want AI inside your social life — Muse Spark.

**In raw performance:** Gemini 3 is currently ahead. Meta is honest about this.

**In social context:** No comparison. Muse Spark has three billion people's social data. Gemini does not.

---

### Meta Muse Spark vs Claude AI

**Claude AI** is made by Anthropic. It is the AI most loved by developers, writers, and professionals. Claude is known for being extremely accurate, honest, and safe to use. In the enterprise world — companies using AI for serious work — Claude is the second most popular AI after ChatGPT.

Claude AI is a focused, serious AI assistant. You use it when you need thoughtful, careful responses. Long documents. Complex analysis. Code. Writing.

**Muse Spark** is going in a completely different direction. It is not trying to be the most careful AI for professionals. It is trying to be the AI that understands your personal life — your style, your friends, your health, your shopping preferences.

They serve different audiences:
- Claude AI → Professionals, developers, writers who need powerful and accurate AI
- Muse Spark → Everyday users who want AI woven into their social apps

**In accuracy and reasoning for professional tasks:** Claude AI is still stronger.

**In knowing your personal social world:** Muse Spark is in a completely different league.

## So Why Is Meta Calling This "Superintelligence"?

This is the big word that has everyone talking.

Meta keeps using the phrase **personal superintelligence** when describing where Muse Spark is heading.

What does superintelligence mean?

The simple version: A superintelligent AI is one that is smarter than any human in every possible way. It can solve any problem, learn anything, and do everything better than we can.

We do not have that yet. Not ChatGPT. Not Gemini. Not Claude AI. Not Muse Spark.

So why is Meta using that word?

Because they are describing the **destination** — not where they are today.

Meta is saying: Muse Spark is our first step on the road to personal superintelligence. An AI that does not just answer your questions — but truly understands your world. Your relationships. Your health. Your preferences. Your life.

An AI that is not generic. Not the same for everyone. But personalised to you — built on the actual context of your life.

That is the goal.

Muse Spark today is the foundation. The next model — which Meta says is already in development — will be bigger and more powerful. And each generation builds on the last.

---

## Is Muse Spark Actually Superintelligent Today?

No. Let us be honest.

Muse Spark is a genuinely impressive new AI. But it is not the most powerful AI model available right now.

Meta itself acknowledges that in coding and some complex tasks, Muse Spark is behind GPT-5, Gemini 3, and Claude AI.

What Muse Spark does have is something none of the others have:

- Three billion people's social data
- Deep integration into the most-used apps in the world
- A shopping mode that is genuinely unique
- The ability to understand the visual world around you

That is a different kind of advantage. Not pure intelligence — but context. And in many ways, context is what makes an AI actually useful in real life.

The most intelligent AI in the world is not very useful if it does not understand anything about you. Muse Spark knows your world because it is built on it.

## One Big Warning — Privacy

This needs to be said clearly.

Muse Spark's biggest advantage is also its biggest concern.

The reason it can personalise your experience and power the shopping mode is because Meta has access to everything you do on Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp.

Every post you like. Every creator you follow. Every product you tap on.

Meta's privacy policy sets **few limits** on how the company can use data you share with its AI system. That is different from ChatGPT or Claude AI — where your conversations are not connected to a massive social media platform.

If you are comfortable with Meta having access to your data — Muse Spark offers something genuinely new and useful.

If you are not — you might prefer keeping your AI life separate from your social life.

This is a choice only you can make. But you should make it knowing the trade-off.
